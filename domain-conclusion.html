Conclusion and Future Work

Conclusion

Agri-Sense successfully integrates edge AI, robotic intervention, and SDN-driven resilient networking into one autonomous greenhouse platform. It closes the gap between passive monitoring and direct agricultural action.

Practical Impact

The system improves intervention reliability in low-connectivity environments, reduces chemical waste, and lowers dependence on continuous manual labor while maintaining crop safety.

Future Work

Planned extensions include solar-powered sensor nodes for long-term energy autonomy, multi-robot coordination for large greenhouse deployments, and expanded perception for pest density and soil nutrient deficiency analysis.

Current Scope and Limitations

The current validation is based on controlled greenhouse trials and targeted disease classes. Future validation will expand to larger deployment scales, broader disease variability, and long-duration seasonal robustness testing.
